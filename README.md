# HR Employee Attrition Analytics Dashboard – Power BI

An interactive Power BI dashboard analyzing employee attrition across departments, demographics, and job factors — built to identify the strongest drivers of employee turnover using DAX, Power Query, and Power BI's AI-powered Key Influencers visual.

---

## 📊 Dashboard Preview

### 🏠 Page 1 – Executive Overview
Total employees, attrition rate, average tenure, average income, attrition split, attrition by department, and attrition trend by hire year.

![Executive Overview](Images/ExecutiveOverview.png)

### 👥 Page 2 – Demographics & Attrition
Attrition rate by age group and tenure bucket, a department-vs-job-role matrix, and interactive filters (Gender, Marital Status, Department).

![Demographics](Images/Demographics.png)

### 🤖 Page 3 – Correlation & Predictive Insights
Attrition rate by overtime, job satisfaction, and work-life balance, a salary-vs-experience scatter plot, and the Power BI Key Influencers AI visual.

![Predictive Insights](Images/PredictiveInsights.png)

## ✨ Features

**Executive Overview**
- Total Employees, Attrition Rate, Avg Tenure, Avg Monthly Income (KPI cards)
- Employee Attrition Distribution (donut chart)
- Attrition Rate by Department (bar chart)
- Attrition Trend by Hire Year (line chart)

**Demographics & Attrition**
- Attrition Rate by Age Group
- Attrition Rate by Tenure Bucket
- Department vs Job Role Attrition Matrix
- Interactive filters: Gender, Marital Status, Department

**Correlation & Predictive Insights**
- Attrition Rate by Overtime
- Attrition Rate by Job Satisfaction
- Attrition Rate by Work-Life Balance
- Salary vs Experience scatter plot (Monthly Income vs Years at Company)
- Power BI Key Influencers AI visual — automatically ranks the strongest factors behind attrition

---

## 📈 Key Insights
- Sales and IT have the highest attrition rates; Finance the lowest.
- Employees under 25 and over 55 churn at the highest rates.
- Employees with less than 1 year of tenure are the most likely to leave.
- Overtime is one of the strongest predictors of attrition.
- Low job satisfaction and poor work-life balance both correlate strongly with attrition.
- The Key Influencers visual confirms age, overtime, and job satisfaction as top drivers.
